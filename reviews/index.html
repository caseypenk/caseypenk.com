casey penk

reviews


movies

spider-man: no way home (2021)

already-dated cgi, throwaway one-liners, and an over-reliance on the marvel affiliation mar what should have been the ultimate spider-man crossover and cheapen what should have felt like a stunning and unprecedented use of the most legendary actors from the franchise’s back catalog but instead felt awkward and anticlimactic

matrix resurrections (2021)

nostalgic in the first quarter, metaphysical in the second quarter, and excessively violent in the very long second half, during which i aged 40 years and underwent multiple psychotic breaks

don't look up (2021)

nihilistic invective against anti-science acolytes; every actor was better in another movie, except timothée chalamet, who brings something unique and beautiful to every single role he plays, including this (literally) earth-shattering performance as a christian delivering what are essentially the last rites of the human species

dune (2021)

i spontaneously began crying at several points in the movie, due both to the astoundingly gorgeous cinematography and to the civilization-scale injustice that manages to make our human crises feel both manageable by comparison but also like "holy war, spreading across the universe like unquenchable fire"; timothée chalamet carries the entire movie but is supported by the most well-rounded and capable cast of any sci-fi movie since interstellar or even blade runner

games

ocarina of time (1998)

an unparalleled masterpiece still stunning in its scope, ambition, narrative eloquence, thematic resonance, and cohesive lore, becomes the greatest game of all time when combined with koji kondo’s eternal soundtrack
